A.P.J Abdul Kalam

1931 - 2015

Missile Men of India

Dr. APJ Abdul Kalam, fondly known as the Missile Man of India, was an inspiring scientist, visionary leader, and the 11th President of India. Born on October 15, 1931, in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu, he rose from humble beginnings to become a key figure in India's defense and space programs. His contributions to the development of India's ballistic missile technology, including the Agni and Prithvi missiles, earned him national and international recognition. As President (2002-2007), he was admired for his humility, wisdom, and dedication to the country's progress. Beyond science and politics, he was a passionate advocate for education and youth empowerment, encouraging students to dream big and work hard. His books, such as Wings of Fire and Ignited Minds, continue to inspire generations. Dr. Kalam’s legacy is one of perseverance, innovation, and selfless service, making him a true role model for millions around the world.

Biographies

  • Born on October 15, 1931, in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu, India.
  • Pursued Aerospace Engineering at Madras Institute of Technology (MIT), Chennai.
  • Moved to ISRO (1969) and led India's first Satellite Launch Vehicle (SLV-III) in 1980.
  • Elected as the 11th President of India with massive support.
  • Believed in Vision 2020 – a roadmap for making India a developed nation.
